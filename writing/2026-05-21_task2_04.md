## 문제

Some people believe that governments should invest more money in public transportation rather than building more roads. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

---

## 모범 답안

Whether governments should prioritise investment in public transportation over building more roads is widely debated. I strongly agree with this view, as expanding public transit would help preserve the environment and ease urban congestion.

First, investing in public transportation can significantly reduce carbon emissions. In areas where public transit is limited, people have no choice but to rely on private cars, contributing to air pollution. In contrast, cities like Seoul and Tokyo have demonstrated that well-funded metro systems can significantly reduce car usage and, consequently, carbon emissions. By expanding public transportation networks, governments can encourage more people to shift away from private vehicles and help address the growing threat of climate change.

Secondly, improved public transportation can ease urban congestion. Many city centres suffer from severe traffic congestion, partly because existing public transit systems are overcrowded or lack sufficient coverage in residential areas. When commuters cannot find a convenient public transit option near their homes, they are forced to rely on cars, worsening congestion. If governments invest in expanding metro lines and bus routes, more residents would have reliable access to public transit, reducing the number of private cars on the road.

In conclusion, government investment in public transportation is far more beneficial than building additional roads. Expanding transit networks would not only reduce air pollution but also create more liveable and sustainable cities for future generations.
