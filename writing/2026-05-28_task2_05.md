## 문제

Some people think that it is better for children to begin learning a foreign language at primary school rather than secondary school. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

---

## 답안 (250~280단어 목표)
Whether learning second language in primary school is better for children is widely debated topic. I strongly agree that primary school is the proper age to learn foreign language, as they can acquire language naturally and they have already developed a solid foundation in their mother tongue. 

First, children at primary school age absorb information more easily. They learn faster than older learners, furthermore, as they can acquire language naturally, retaining language skills is more effective. Unlike secondary school, where students are primarily focused on academic subjects, primary school offers an environment where language can be absorbed naturally. For example, I saw a video on Youtube which was about the person who had lived in Korea when he was young and over 50 years old, can speak Korean fluently. He said, "Having lived in Korea in young age for so long, I still remember the language and accent."

Secondly, primary school students having already developed a solid foundation in their mother tongue. With this foundation, they can expand their language coverage. They can learn simultaneosly and this would be a effective method to acquire new language. If learning foreign language in younger age than primary school, they can only acquire basic words, because their language foundation is not completed. 

In conclusion, learning foreign language in primary school age is proper period, as they absorb easily than older age, and they can understand the language than younger age.

## 모범 답안

Whether children should begin learning a foreign language at primary school rather than secondary school is a widely debated topic. I strongly agree with this view, as young learners can acquire language more naturally and have already developed a solid foundation in their mother tongue.

First, children at primary school age absorb information more easily than older learners. Unlike secondary school, where students are primarily focused on academic subjects, primary school offers an environment where a foreign language can be acquired naturally through daily exposure. As a result, children are able to retain language skills far more effectively than those who begin at a later stage. Research has shown that individuals who are exposed to a second language in early childhood tend to develop near-native pronunciation and fluency, skills that become significantly harder to achieve in adulthood.

Secondly, primary school students have already developed a solid foundation in their mother tongue, making them well-equipped to take on a second language simultaneously. While some may argue that introducing a foreign language at this stage could cause confusion, studies suggest that children who are bilingual from an early age demonstrate stronger cognitive flexibility and problem-solving skills. Learning a second language at primary school therefore not only builds linguistic ability but also enhances overall academic development.

In conclusion, I strongly believe that primary school is the most suitable period for children to begin learning a foreign language, as they absorb language more naturally and already possess the linguistic foundation needed to support simultaneous learning.
