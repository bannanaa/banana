
# IELTS Writing Task 2
> 날짜: 2026-05-20 | 목표 시간: 40분

## 문제

Some people think that technology is making people less sociable and more isolated. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

---

## 답안 (250~280단어 목표)
Whether technology is making people less sociable and more isolated is widely debated. I strongly agree that this is indeed the case. 
On the one hand, when smartphones were introduced, it was an alternative method to telephone, which only supported voice calls. However, as wireless technology has advanced, people can use internet on the phone. Nowadays most individuals have their own cell phone and people can connect with friends even strangers online. They share every usual thing in their online space and this makes individuals meet friends. Although people may have hundreds of online followers, these connections are not genuine relationships, which can lead to feelings of isolation. 
On the other hand, following the pandemic, this phenomenon has been accelerated. During the pandemic many things changed to contactless method. For example, many restaurants started to deliver and delivery applications  boomed. Furthermore, people began buying things online and get the product in front of home. In this period, people have become accustomed to not meeting other people. They have grown comfortable with contactless interactions. Actually, many people are losing the ability to communicate. 
In conclusion, as technology advances, it is becoming easy to connect with others. However the connection is weakening because their bond is not healthy. We should strive to build a healthy bond and connection with others.

## 모범 답안

Whether technology is making people less sociable and more isolated is widely debated. I strongly agree that this is indeed the case, as digital communication is gradually replacing meaningful face-to-face interaction.

First, the rise of smartphones has fundamentally changed the way people connect. While mobile phones were originally introduced as an alternative to landline telephones, advances in wireless technology have transformed them into devices that enable constant online communication. Although people can now connect with friends and even strangers across the world, these relationships often lack depth. People may have hundreds of online followers, yet feel isolated, because these connections are not genuine relationships built on shared experience.

Furthermore, following the pandemic, this phenomenon has been significantly accelerated. During this period, many aspects of daily life shifted to contactless methods — restaurants offered delivery services, delivery applications boomed, and people began shopping online rather than visiting stores in person. As a result, people have grown increasingly accustomed to not meeting others face-to-face, and many are gradually losing the ability to communicate effectively in person.

In conclusion, while technology makes it easier than ever to connect with others, the quality of these connections is weakening. We should strive to build genuine bonds with those around us, rather than relying solely on digital interaction.
