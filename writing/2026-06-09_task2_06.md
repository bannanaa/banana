## 문제

Some people think that the best way to reduce crime is to give longer prison sentences. Others, however, believe there are better alternative ways of reducing crime. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

**유형:** Discuss both views + give opinion
**목표 단어 수:** 250~280단어

## 답안 (250~280단어 목표)

Discuss both views -> prison sentence 를 늘리는것이 reduce crime 에 partially influence, however, this method is not 근본적인 solusion. 근본적으로는 교육을 통해 재발되지 않도록 인성을 변화시켜야 한다. 

Whether longer prison sentences are the most effective way to reduce crime is a widely debated topic. While some believe that stricter penalties act as a deterrent, others argue that alternative approaches are more effective. In my view, longer sentences can partly reduce crime, but this alone do not address the root cause. 

On the one hand, proponents of longer prison sentences argue that extended incarceration can deter crime to some extent. Prisoners who serve longer sentences may perceive their time in prison as a genuine punishment, which can motivate them not to reoffend upon release. Furthermore, keeping offenders imprisoned for longer periods prevents them from committing further crimes during that time.

On the other hand, longer prison sentences alone do not address the root cause. To reduce the crime rate, prisons should educate and rehabilitate prisoners and address their mental health. Research on recidivism suggests that individuals with social or psychological problems and a criminal record are significantly more likely to reoffend than those with no prior convictions.

In conclusion, longer prison sentences could help to partly reduce crime while offenders are incarcerated; however, this is not a fundamental solution. To tackle the crime rate effectively, prisons should educate and rehabilitate prisoners and address their mental health.

## 모범 답안

Whether longer prison sentences are the most effective way to reduce crime is a widely debated issue. While some believe that stricter penalties deter criminal behaviour, others argue that alternative approaches are more effective. In my view, longer sentences may offer a short-term benefit, but they alone do not address the root cause of crime.

On the one hand, proponents of longer prison sentences argue that extended incarceration can reduce crime to some extent. Offenders who serve longer sentences may perceive imprisonment as a genuine punishment, which can motivate them not to reoffend upon release. Furthermore, keeping criminals incarcerated for longer periods prevents them from committing further offences during that time.

On the other hand, simply extending prison sentences fails to tackle the underlying causes of criminal behaviour. Research on recidivism suggests that individuals with social or psychological problems are significantly more likely to reoffend after release than those without such issues. Therefore, prisons should place greater emphasis on educating and rehabilitating inmates, as well as addressing their mental health needs, in order to break the cycle of reoffending.

In conclusion, while longer prison sentences can reduce crime in the short term by keeping offenders off the streets, this is not a fundamental solution. A more effective approach would be to invest in education and rehabilitation programmes within prisons, targeting the root causes that drive individuals to commit crime in the first place.
