## 2026-05-08 (Task2 01 — Sports facilities & public health)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `advocate` (n) | 지지자, 옹호자 | *Advocates of this approach contend that it is effective.* |
| `contend` (v) | 주장하다 | *Proponents contend that sports facilities lower the barrier to exercise.* |
| `proponent` (n) | 지지자, 찬성론자 | *Proponents of sports facilities argue that they are accessible.* |
| `drawback` (n) | 단점, 결점 | *There are significant drawbacks to relying solely on this approach.* |
| `solely` (adv) | 오로지, ~만 | *We should not rely solely on sports facilities.* |
| `leading to passive` (표현) | 수동적인 참여로 이어지는 | *...leading to passive rather than active participation.* ← 통째로 외우기 |
| `affordable` (adj) | 저렴한, 부담 없는 | *Affordable group fitness classes are a more effective solution.* |
| `alongside` (prep) | ~와 함께, ~옆에서 | *When people exercise alongside peers, they stay more motivated.* |
| `invest in` (표현) | ~에 투자하다 | *Investing in community-based programmes yields lasting results.* |

---

### 헷갈리기 쉬운 것
- `advocate` vs `proponent` — 둘 다 "지지자"지만 번갈아 쓰면 어휘 다양성 점수 ↑
- `contend` vs `argue` — 같은 뜻, 번갈아 쓸 것
- `solely` = only 의 격식체 버전 → IELTS에서 `only` 대신 쓰면 좋음
- `affordable` = cheap의 격식체 → `cheap` 대신 항상 이걸로

## 2026-05-15 (Task2 02 — Compulsory community service)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `on their own initiative` (표현) | 스스로의 의지로 | *Few students choose to volunteer on their own initiative.* |
| `be exposed to ~` (표현) | ~에 노출되다 | *Students are exposed to people outside their usual social circles.* |
| `broaden one's horizons` (표현) | 시야를 넓히다 | *Community service broadens students' horizons.* |
| `foster social awareness` (표현) | 사회적 인식을 기르다 | *Volunteering helps foster social awareness among teenagers.* |
| `go beyond what the classroom can offer` (표현) | 교실에서 배울 수 없는 것을 경험하다 | *Community service offers experiences that go beyond what the classroom can offer.* |
| `regardless of` (표현) | ~에 관계없이 | *All students should volunteer, regardless of their personal motivation.* |
| `compulsory` (adj) | 의무적인, 필수의 | *Making community service compulsory ensures equal participation.* |
| `empathy` (n) | 공감 능력 | *Volunteering helps students develop empathy and a broader worldview.* |

---

### 헷갈리기 쉬운 것
- `social issues` (O) vs `society issues` (X) — "사회적 문제"는 반드시 **social** issues
- `an effective` (O) vs `a effective` (X) — 모음 앞에는 an
- `police station` (O) vs `police office` (X)
- `affect` (동사) vs `effect` (명사) — "영향을 미치다"는 **affect**

## 2026-05-21 (Task2 03 — Technology & social isolation)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `face-to-face interaction` (표현) | 대면 상호작용 | *Digital communication is replacing meaningful face-to-face interaction.* |
| `genuine relationships` (표현) | 진정한 관계 | *Online connections are often not genuine relationships.* |
| `lead to feelings of isolation` (표현) | 고립감으로 이어지다 | *Shallow online connections can lead to feelings of isolation.* |
| `this phenomenon has been accelerated` (표현) | 이 현상이 가속화되었다 | *Following the pandemic, this phenomenon has been significantly accelerated.* |
| `accustomed to ~ing` (표현) | ~하는 것에 익숙해지다 | *People have grown accustomed to not meeting others face-to-face.* |
| `contactless methods` (표현) | 비대면 방식 | *Daily life shifted to contactless methods during the pandemic.* |
| `lose the ability to communicate` (표현) | 소통 능력을 잃다 | *Many people are gradually losing the ability to communicate in person.* |
| `sociable` (adj) | 사교적인 | *Technology is making people less sociable and more isolated.* |

---

### 헷갈리기 쉬운 것
- `On the other hand` (O) vs `On the second hand` (X)
- `boom` — 자동사이므로 `was boomed` (X) → `boomed` (O)
- `accustomed to not meeting` (O) vs `accustomed not to meet` (X) — to 뒤는 동명사
- `during the pandemic` (O) vs `in pandemic` (X) — 관사 필수

## 2026-05-26 (Task2 04 — Public transportation vs roads)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `ease urban congestion` (표현) | 도시 혼잡을 완화하다 | *Investing in public transit would ease urban congestion.* |
| `carbon emissions` (표현) | 탄소 배출 | *Well-funded metro systems significantly reduce carbon emissions.* |
| `are forced to rely on cars` (표현) | 자동차에 의존할 수밖에 없다 | *Without public transit, people are forced to rely on private cars.* |
| `shift away from private vehicles` (표현) | 자가용 이용에서 벗어나다 | *Governments can encourage people to shift away from private vehicles.* |
| `liveable and sustainable cities` (표현) | 살기 좋고 지속 가능한 도시 | *Investment in transit creates liveable and sustainable cities.* |
| `residential areas` (표현) | 주거 지역 | *Public transit often lacks sufficient coverage in residential areas.* |
| `lead to a reduction in` (표현) | ~의 감소로 이어지다 | *This would lead to a reduction in air pollution.* |
| `prioritise A over B` (표현) | B보다 A를 우선시하다 | *Governments should prioritise public transit over building roads.* |

---

### 헷갈리기 쉬운 것
- `invest in` (O) vs `invest to` (X) — invest 뒤는 반드시 **in**
- `lead to a reduction` (O) vs `lead to reduction` (X) — 관사 a 필수
- `although` 오타 주의 — `althought` (X)
- `many trains` (O) vs `much trains` (X) — 셀 수 있으면 many

## 2026-06-02 (Task2 05 — Foreign language at primary school)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `absorb information more easily` (표현) | 정보를 더 쉽게 흡수하다 | *Children at primary school age absorb information more easily.* |
| `retain language skills` (표현) | 언어 능력을 유지하다 | *Young learners retain language skills far more effectively.* |
| `solid foundation in their mother tongue` (표현) | 모국어의 탄탄한 기반 | *Children have already developed a solid foundation in their mother tongue.* |
| `Unlike A, where..., B offers...` (표현) | A와 달리 B는 ~을 제공한다 | *Unlike secondary school, where students focus on academics, primary school offers a natural environment.* |
| `at a young age` (표현) | 어린 나이에 | *Children exposed to a second language at a young age develop near-native fluency.* |

---

- `an effective` (O) vs `a effective` (X) — 반복 실수, 완전히 잡기
- `language skills` (O) vs `language coverage` (X)
- `at a young age` (O) vs `in young age` (X) — 전치사 at + 관사 a

## 2026-06-12 (Task2 06 — Prison sentences & rehabilitation)

| 단어/표현 | 뜻 | 예문 |
|----------|-----|------|
| `deter` / `deterrent` (v/n) | 억제하다 / 억제 수단 | *Longer sentences may act as a deterrent to potential offenders.* |
| `incarceration` (n) | 수감, 구금 | *Extended incarceration alone does not reduce reoffending.* |
| `recidivism` (n) | 재범(률) | *Research on recidivism suggests rehabilitation is more effective.* |
| `reoffend` (v) | 재범하다 | *Prisoners with mental health issues are more likely to reoffend.* |
| `rehabilitate` / `rehabilitation` (v/n) | 교화하다 / 재활 | *Prisons should focus on rehabilitating offenders.* |
| `reintegrate into society` (표현) | 사회에 재통합하다 | *Education helps prisoners reintegrate into society after release.* |
| `address the root cause` (표현) | 근본 원인을 해결하다 | *Longer sentences alone do not address the root cause of crime.* |
| `prior convictions` (표현) | 전과 | *Those with no prior convictions are less likely to reoffend.* |
| `tackle crime at its root` (표현) | 범죄를 근본부터 해결하다 | *We must tackle crime at its root through education.* |
| `vocational training` (표현) | 직업 훈련 | *Vocational training in prison improves employment prospects upon release.* |

---

### 헷갈리기 쉬운 것
- `deter` vs `prevent` — deter는 심리적 억제, prevent는 물리적 방지
- `recidivism` 발음 — [rɪˈsɪdɪvɪzəm] 외워두기, IELTS 고득점 어휘
- `rehabilitate` = "교화하다" — `educate`와 함께 세트로 쓸 것 (`educate and rehabilitate`)
